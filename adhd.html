Understanding ADHD

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder that affects attention, impulsivity, and hyperactivity. It is commonly diagnosed in children but can also affect adults. There are three main types of ADHD:

  • Inattentive Type
  • Hyperactive-Impulsive Type
  • Combined Type

Symptoms of ADHD

Common symptoms of ADHD include:

  • Difficulty sustaining attention
  • Impulsivity
  • Hyperactivity
  • Disorganization
  • Forgetfulness

Early Signs

Teens:

  • Forgetting to hand in homework or handing it in late
  • Trouble staying focused when they are supposed too
  • Getting bored easily or not engaging in things they aren't interested in
  • Procrastination often or doing assignments at the last minute.
  • Getting to overwhelmed to even start assignments.
  • Not being able to sleep until really exhausted.
  • Being late to school and having a hard time with time management.
